Chiral materials possess unique properties due to their asymmetry, making them non-superimposable on their mirror images. These materials play a crucial role in various fields, including pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, flavors, fragrances, and materials science. Their significance in enantioselective processes, where the two enantiomers of a chiral molecule exhibit different biological activities, underscores their importance in modern chemistry and industry. End-Use Industries:

Key end-use industries for chiral materials include:

Pharmaceuticals: The pharmaceutical industry is the largest consumer of chiral materials, utilizing them in the production of enantiomerically pure drugs. Chiral drugs often exhibit enhanced efficacy and reduced side effects compared to their racemic counterparts.
Agrochemicals: In the agrochemical industry, chiral materials are used in the formulation of pesticides and herbicides, where enantiomeric purity can significantly impact the biological activity and environmental profile of the products.
Flavors and Fragrances: Chiral materials are critical in the flavors and fragrances industry, where enantiomers can have distinct sensory properties, influencing the taste and smell of products.
Materials Science: Chiral materials find applications in advanced materials science, including the development of chiral polymers and nanomaterials with unique optical and electronic properties.
