Plant growth hormones, also known as phytohormones, are essential chemicals that regulate various functions in plants, including growth, development, and responses to environmental stimuli. Major classes of plant growth hormones include auxins, cytokinins, gibberellins, ethylene, and abscisic acid. These hormones are widely used in agriculture, horticulture, and plant biotechnology to enhance crop yields, improve plant health, and manage growth. End-Use Industries:

Key end-use industries for plant growth hormones include:

Agriculture: The agricultural industry is the largest consumer of plant growth hormones, utilizing them to enhance crop yields, improve plant health, and manage growth. Phytohormones like auxins, cytokinins, and gibberellins are widely used to promote root development, stimulate flowering, and increase fruit size.
Horticulture: In the horticulture industry, plant growth hormones are used to improve the growth and quality of ornamental plants, flowers, and vegetables. They are essential for tissue culture, propagation, and the production of high-value horticultural products.
Plant Biotechnology: Plant growth hormones play a crucial role in plant biotechnology, where they are used in genetic engineering, tissue culture, and the development of transgenic plants. These hormones help in the regeneration of plants from tissue cultures and enhance the efficiency of biotechnological processes.
Turf Management: In turf management, plant growth hormones are used to maintain healthy lawns, sports fields, and golf courses. They help in promoting root growth, improving stress tolerance, and maintaining the aesthetic quality of turfgrass.
