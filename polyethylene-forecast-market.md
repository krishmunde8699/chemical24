End-Use Industries:

Key end-use industries for polyethylene include:

Packaging: The packaging industry is the largest consumer of polyethylene, utilizing it for producing films, bags, bottles, and containers. Its properties such as flexibility, durability, and cost-effectiveness make it ideal for packaging applications.
Construction: In the construction industry, polyethylene is used in applications such as pipes, insulation, and geomembranes due to its strength, durability, and resistance to moisture and chemicals.
Automotive: The automotive industry uses polyethylene for manufacturing various components, including fuel tanks, bumpers, and interior parts, benefiting from its lightweight, durability, and impact resistance.
Consumer Goods: Polyethylene is widely used in the production of consumer goods such as household items, toys, and appliances, owing to its versatility and ease of processing.
