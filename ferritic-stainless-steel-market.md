Ferritic stainless steel is a type of stainless steel that is primarily composed of iron and chromium, with lower amounts of carbon and other alloying elements compared to austenitic stainless steel. Known for its magnetic properties, corrosion resistance, and cost-effectiveness, ferritic stainless steel is widely used in automotive exhaust systems, household appliances, industrial equipment, and architectural applications.
